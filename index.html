Deep Benchmarking Analysis of Talent Strategies

Sportswear Brands in the China Market (2025)
Executive Summary

In 2025, competition in China's sports and outdoor market has fundamentally evolved—from battles over marketing and distribution channels into a decisive confrontation over organizational capability and talent models. This report delivers an in-depth analysis of the talent strategy fundamentals of seven core competitors:

  • ANTA has built a highly defensible and difficult-to-replicate "symbiotic" talent system. Through internal cultivation and deeply embedded welfare mechanisms, it has established an exceptionally stable talent foundation.
  • adidas has created an outstanding employee experience through its "homecourt" culture and strengthened its retail talent pipeline via accelerated development programs, thereby reinforcing its local roots.
  • LI-NING demonstrates extreme business agility, leveraging highly flexible incentive mechanisms and flat decision-making structures to attract execution-oriented, results-driven talent.
  • Emerging brands such as On are diverting top-tier "product-believer" innovators by appealing to pure professional conviction and ultra-flat organizational structures.
  • Xtep has vertically entrenched itself in the running category, where its ecosystem-based approach creates unique cohesion among highly specialized professionals.
  • PEAK's recent strategic adjustments reflect an efficiency-first mindset; the resulting talent mobility may generate selective recruitment opportunities in specific functional areas.

For Nike, the core challenge is clear: its globally standardized systems are increasingly insufficient when responding to local agility demands, deepening category specialization, and meeting the new expectations of talent—namely value alignment and accelerated growth.

In response, this report proposes the "Pathfinder Program", recommending that Nike immediately initiate an inside-out organizational transformation through a three-phase approach:

  1. Establishing critical expert roles
  2. Creating innovation special zones
  3. Reframing its employer narrative

The objective is to decisively win the talent war of the next five years.

Key Conclusions

The current competitive landscape represents an ultimate contest of organizational models.

  • ANTA cultivates "insiders" through systems.
  • adidas unites "strivers" through culture.
  • On attracts "believers" through conviction.

Nike must resolve the inherent paradox between "global standards" and "local agility." The answer is not simple poaching, but rather the deliberate design of a more compelling new working model for its target talent segments.

1. ANTA Group vs. Nike: Systemic Symbiosis vs. Brand Halo

ANTA has built an internally generated talent system that is exceptionally difficult to replicate. Its core is not aggressive external hiring through premium compensation, but the long-cycle cultivation of backbone talent imbued with "ANTA DNA" over a ten-year horizon.

Through ultra-long-term investment, ANTA aligns individual career trajectories with corporate growth, creating resonance over time. Core Distinction: ANTA is a system-driven ecosystem builder, while Nike is a brand-driven innovation leader. ANTA secures its talent foundation through internal cultivation and deep welfare integration; Nike attracts elite talent through global brand equity and innovation culture.

Talent Acquisition Strategy for Nike

Challenge: ANTA has cultivated a cohort of highly loyal mid-to-senior leaders fluent in complex multi-brand operations. They are deeply embedded and resistant to conventional role- or pay-based poaching.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Target: Select individuals within premium brands (e.g., Arc'teryx, DESCENTE) who possess international exposure or aspire to a broader innovation platform.
  • Value Proposition: Offer a leap from operating a successful brand to shaping the future of global sport. Leverage Nike's frontier technology initiatives and unmatched global influence to attract those who feel constrained by systemic rigidity and seek larger, more ambitious challenges.
2. adidas vs. Nike: Cultural Belonging vs. Elite Performance Orientation

adidas has cultivated a strong sense of local belonging by creating best-in-class office environments and an inclusive culture. Its talent strategy is designed to attract and retain professionals who are willing to grow and strive within a structured system.

Repeatedly recognized as a "China Top Employer," signaling long-term investment in the China market and appealing to candidates seeking a stable global platform. The halo of a global innovation benchmark. Exceptionally attractive to top-tier design, marketing, and product innovation talent—symbolizing the pinnacle of the industry. Core Distinction: adidas is a model student of localization, while Nike is a global leader. adidas seeks deep integration into China; Nike seeks to lead China.

Talent Acquisition Strategy for Nike

Challenge: In functions such as retail management, supply chain, and local marketing, adidas offers a platform comparable to Nike's—but with a gentler culture and clearer, more predictable career paths.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Target: Frontline retail trainers, regional retail managers, and senior designers and product marketers from the Shanghai design center.
  • Value Proposition: For talent seeking faster breakthroughs and higher innovation ceilings, emphasize Nike's culture of challenging conventions and steeper growth curves—backed by more demanding, high-impact projects and greater creative latitude.
3. LI-NING vs. Nike: Agility and Flexibility vs. Systemic Standardization

LI-NING represents the most agile and execution-driven organizational archetype among China's domestic sportswear brands. Its talent strategy is explicitly designed to support rapid decision-making, high-frequency iteration, and immediate market responsiveness.

Dimension LI-NING Nike Implications and Challenges for Nike

Strategy is formulated through multilayered global processes, ensuring consistency but reducing responsiveness. Challenge: Nike's decision-making cadence often lags behind fast-moving local trends. Against LI-NING, speed itself becomes a competitive disadvantage.
Organizational Structure Highly flattened. Clear accountability, minimal hierarchy, and strong empowerment of frontline teams. Matrix-based global organization. Multiple reporting lines and approval layers ensure alignment but increase friction. Incentive Mechanisms Strong performance linkage. Bonuses and promotions are tightly coupled with short-term business outcomes, creating a high-pressure, high-reward environment. Balanced incentive design. Performance is evaluated through a mix of financial results, leadership behaviors, and long-term impact. Challenge: For execution-oriented talent, Nike's incentive feedback may feel distant or abstract compared with LI-NING's immediate rewards.
Talent Attraction Appeals to doers and operators. Highly attractive to individuals who value autonomy, speed, and tangible results over process sophistication. Appeals to strategists and innovators. Strong draw for talent seeking structured development and global exposure. Insight: Nike should differentiate its EVP by clearly signaling which roles offer true end-to-end ownership and rapid decision authority.

Core Distinction: LI-NING is a battlefield organization, optimized for speed and execution; Nike is a system organization, optimized for scale, consistency, and long-term brand leadership.

Talent Acquisition Strategy for Nike

Challenge: Execution-focused professionals may perceive Nike as slow-moving, overly process-heavy, and distant from frontline business impact.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Target: High-performing category managers, channel operators, and growth marketers who have proven their ability to scale success beyond rapid wins.
  • Value Proposition: Position Nike as the platform where battle-hardened executors can elevate into system builders—moving from short-term victories to shaping enduring brands and categories at scale.
4. On vs. Nike: Belief-Driven Organization vs. System-Driven Organization

On represents a new-generation sportswear brand whose talent strategy is built almost entirely on professional belief and product conviction. Compared with traditional giants, its organizational model is deliberately minimalist, relying on shared values rather than institutional density.

Dimension On Nike Implications and Challenges for Nike

Institutional excellence and global leadership. Talent is attracted by systems, resources, and global influence. Challenge: On competes for the same top-tier product and innovation talent, but with a radically different emotional proposition—belief over structure.
Organizational Structure Ultra-flat and founder-influenced. Decision-making is highly concentrated among senior product and brand leaders. Multi-layered global matrix. Decisions balance global alignment and local execution. Insight: Nike's layered structure may dilute product passion. Creating small, founder-like teams within Nike could restore emotional ownership.
Talent Motivation Intrinsic motivation dominates. Individuals are driven by craftsmanship, innovation, and the desire to build something meaningful from the ground up. Extrinsic and intrinsic motivations coexist. Compensation, brand prestige, and innovation opportunities are balanced. Employer Appeal A cult-like attraction among elite product innovators. On is perceived as authentic, pure, and uncompromised. A benchmark platform for industry excellence. Nike represents the pinnacle of professional achievement. Insight: Nike must articulate how scale and belief can coexist—innovation with impact, not innovation diluted.

Core Distinction: On is a belief-centric organization—small, focused, and conviction-driven. Nike is a system-centric organization—powerful, scalable, and institutionally robust.

Talent Acquisition Strategy for Nike

Challenge: Nike risks losing elite product innovators who seek purity, autonomy, and deep emotional connection to the product.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Target: Senior designers, advanced materials experts, and category innovation leaders who desire scale without surrendering belief.
  • Value Proposition: Offer belief at scale—the chance to turn conviction into global impact, supported by unmatched R&D, athlete insight, and platform power.
5. Xtep vs. Nike: Vertical Specialization vs. Horizontal Expansion

Xtep has deliberately chosen a highly focused vertical strategy, anchoring itself firmly in the running category. Its talent strategy, organizational design, and resource allocation all serve a single objective: becoming the most professional and credible running brand in China.

Dimension Xtep Nike Implications and Challenges for Nike

Insight: For pure running experts, Nike must clarify why a broader platform still offers the deepest running impact.

Talent Acquisition Strategy for Nike

Challenge: Nike risks being perceived as too broad for specialists who seek deep category immersion and visible authority within a single sport.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Target: Senior running footwear designers, performance marketers, and athlete managers seeking global reach beyond China.
  • Value Proposition: Position Nike as the platform where deep specialization meets global amplification—allowing running experts to influence not just one market, but the future of running worldwide.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Value Proposition: Offer a transition from efficiency execution to value creation at scale, enabling operational experts to contribute to long-term brand and innovation strategies.
Core Talent Trends & Nike's Critical Gaps

Based on the brand benchmarking above, talent competition in the 2025 China market exhibits three definitive trends. These trends define the capability gaps Nike must address.

Trend 1: From "Employer Brand" to "Team Model" & "Manager Reputation"

Top talent is now choosing a specific "team atmosphere," "level of empowerment," and "quality of their manager" rather than just a corporate brand.

Nike's Gap: The globally standardized management system may lead to insufficient innovation flexibility and decision-making authority at the front-line team level, weakening its appeal to top hands-on experts.

Trend 2: Shift from "Commercial Generalists" to "Vertical Specialists"

Market growth relies on segmented tracks (running, outdoor, yoga). Brands need "running product experts," "outdoor community operators," "yoga category leads"—not just marketing and sales generalists.

Nike's Gap: The organization is better at cultivating and attracting "generalist" commercial leaders. It may be surpassed by focused brands like On and XTEP in terms of deep understanding and resource network accumulation in specific sports.

Trend 3: From Compensation to "Value Alignment" & "Growth Experience"

Talent particularly cares about: Can my work directly influence the product? Is my professional opinion respected? Can I achieve extraordinary growth alongside the business?

Nike's Gap: Needs to create a clearer, deliverable "impacting the globe" pathway and experience for local Chinese talent, converting brand potential into personal growth momentum.

Layered Talent Strategy Action Plan for Nike China

The core principle for action: Not simply imitating competitors, but designing a more attractive "way of working" targeting the deep-seated needs of desired talent.

1. Immediate Actions (Next 6 Months): Precision Countermeasures, Addressing Pain Points

Establish a "Local Innovation Black Belt" Job Sequence

  • Action: In core categories like running, training, and outdoor, establish "Black Belt Expert" roles independent of traditional job grades. Recruit globally with compensation benchmarked against market top levels.
  • Empowerment: Grant them significant influence in product definition suggestions and independent budgets for localization innovation experiments.
  • Goal: Directly recruit top specialists from On, HOKA, Salomon, or top-tier sports institutions to rapidly strengthen vertical depth.

Launch the "Channel Commander" Program

  • Action: Recruit hands-on experts proficient in omni-channel retail (content, livestreaming, private traffic, offline integration) from Alibaba, Douyin, Xiaohongshu, and emerging DTC brands.
  • Deployment: Form cross-departmental "New Retail Strike Teams" reporting directly to China leadership, responsible for breaking through key operational links.

Introduce a "Key Talent Retention Dialogue" Mechanism

  • Action: HRBPs and business leaders must hold informal quarterly dialogues with core talent, focusing on one question: "What obstacles do we need to remove for you to achieve greater success at Nike?"
  • Goal: Proactive retention, personalized resolution of talent pain points (e.g., redundant processes, insufficient empowerment).

2. System Building Initiatives (6-18 Months): Solidifying Foundations, Building Systems

Build the "China Innovation Lab"

  • Model: Operate as an independent Business Unit (BU) with its own product development, marketing, and financial accounting authority.
  • Mission: Focus on exploring product lines and business models serving the China market (e.g., light-functional series for urban outdoor scenarios).
  • Value: This is the ultimate "special zone" for attracting and retaining top innovative talent. It demonstrates Nike's willingness to conduct fundamental innovation for China.

Upgrade the "Retail Leadership Accelerator"

  • Benchmark: Learn from the accelerated logic of Adidas's "Retail Elite Program," but infuse it with Nike's sports DNA.
  • Design: Compress the 24-month training period into "6 months HQ rotation + 12 months in-store practical experience + 6 months leading a project."
  • Output: Aim to cultivate store managers who understand both brand and business and can lead future smart stores. This is key to countering Adidas's systematic retail talent output.

Construct a "Category Expert Certification System"

  • Design: Collaborate with global sports science teams to design professional certification courses for core China market categories like running, basketball, and soccer.
  • Mandate: Require relevant product, marketing, and sales employees to obtain corresponding category certifications as a prerequisite for promotion.
  • Purpose: Systematically elevate the organization's professional authority in vertical fields, forming an internal knowledge barrier.

3. Long-Term Culture Shaping (18+ Months): Resetting Mindsets, Winning the Future

Refresh the Local Employer Value Proposition (EVP): "In China, Drive Nike's Future"

  • Narrative: Center all employer brand communication around "How China insights influence global products" and "How the China team leads new business models."
  • Case Studies: Annually spotlight 1-2 successful innovation cases (product, marketing, channel) led by the China team on global internal platforms.

Implement "Global-China" Talent Exchange 2.0

  • Upgrade: Evolve the existing rotation program into an exchange where "key roles back each other up."
  • Example: China's Running Category Director could take a role in the HQ Global Running department, while the corresponding HQ expert comes to China for deep market immersion. The goal is to build deep mutual trust and consensus, truly closing the decision-making loop.

Institutionalize Executive Team "Deep Dive" Immersion

  • Requirement: China leadership team must have one "Store Day" or "Community Day" per month (with consumers, store staff, running clubs).
  • Purpose: Ensure top decision-makers continually sense the real market temperature, preventing strategy-execution disconnect.
Conclusion: The Decisive Point Lies in Organizational Restructuring

Competitors have already built unique talent attraction and retention systems around their respective strategies. Nike's advantage lies in its unparalleled brand, resources, and scale. The task now is not to dismantle competitors' systems, but to leverage its own scale to incubate one or even multiple "new organizational modules" within the China market that can rival the most appealing traits of its competitors.

The key to action is not fine-tuning HR policies, but a profound experiment in organizational empowerment. Success will first be reflected in whether, over the next 12-18 months, Nike can attract the top "mavericks" and "specialists" who previously would never have considered joining a large corporation.

Winning the Talent War Requires an "Inside-Out" Revolution

The current market race no longer ends with a single product or marketing victory, but with victory in "organizational models."

Anta has proven the enduring stamina of systematic cultivation, Adidas has demonstrated the cohesive power of localized belonging, and On has illustrated the pure appeal of professional belief. For Nike, the real challenge is not poaching a few individuals from them, but whether it can undergo a profound, inside-out organizational revolution.

The essence of this revolution is: Perfectly integrating the grand vision of a global brand with the agility, depth, and warmth required by the China market.

The proposed action plan is the beginning of this revolution. Its success will be marked by very specific signs: In the coming year, can Nike attract "mavericks" from internet platforms or niche top brands who have previously rejected large companies? Can it successfully retain the local mid-level leaders favored by competitors, who understand both business and sport?

This is not merely an adjustment of human resources strategy, but a reshaping of strategic core capabilities. Action must begin immediately.

执行摘要

2025年，中国运动户外市场的竞争已发生根本性演变——从营销和渠道的争夺，转变为组织能力与人才模式的决战。本报告深度分析了七大核心竞争者在人才战略上的根本差异：

  • 安踏构建了高度防御性、难以复制的"共生型"人才系统。通过内部培养和深度嵌入的福利机制，建立了异常稳固的人才基础。
  • 阿迪达斯通过其"主场"文化创造卓越的员工体验，并通过加速发展项目强化零售人才梯队，从而深化本地根基。
  • 李宁展现出极致的商业敏捷性，利用高度灵活的激励机制和扁平决策结构，吸引执行导向、结果驱动的人才。
  • On等新兴品牌通过纯粹的职业信念和超扁平组织结构，分流顶级的"产品信徒"创新者。
  • 特步垂直深耕跑步品类，其基于生态系统的方法在高度专业化人才中创造了独特的凝聚力。
  • 匹克近期的战略调整反映了效率优先的思维；由此带来的人才流动可能在特定职能领域创造选择性招聘机会。

对于耐克，核心挑战明确：其全球标准化体系在应对本地敏捷性需求、深化品类专业化、以及满足人才新期望（即价值契合和加速成长）方面日益不足。

为此，本报告提出"探路者计划"，建议耐克立即通过三阶段方法启动由内而外的组织转型：

  1. 建立关键专家角色
  2. 创建创新特区
  3. 重构雇主叙事

目标是在未来五年的人才战中取得决定性胜利。

核心结论

当前竞争格局本质上是组织模式的终极较量。

  • 安踏通过系统培育"内部人"。
  • 阿迪达斯通过文化凝聚"奋斗者"。

耐克必须解决"全球标准"与"本地敏捷性"之间的内在矛盾。答案并非简单挖角，而是为目标人才群体精心设计更具吸引力的新型工作模式。
