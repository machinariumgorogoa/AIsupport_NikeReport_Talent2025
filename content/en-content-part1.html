Executive Summary

In 2025, competition in China's sports and outdoor market has fundamentally evolved—from battles over marketing and distribution channels into a decisive confrontation over organizational capability and talent models. This report delivers an in-depth analysis of the talent strategy fundamentals of seven core competitors:

  • ANTA has built a highly defensible and difficult-to-replicate "symbiotic" talent system. Through internal cultivation and deeply embedded welfare mechanisms, it has established an exceptionally stable talent foundation.
  • adidas has created an outstanding employee experience through its "homecourt" culture and strengthened its retail talent pipeline via accelerated development programs, thereby reinforcing its local roots.
  • LI-NING demonstrates extreme business agility, leveraging highly flexible incentive mechanisms and flat decision-making structures to attract execution-oriented, results-driven talent.
  • Emerging brands such as On are diverting top-tier "product-believer" innovators by appealing to pure professional conviction and ultra-flat organizational structures.
  • Xtep has vertically entrenched itself in the running category, where its ecosystem-based approach creates unique cohesion among highly specialized professionals.
  • PEAK's recent strategic adjustments reflect an efficiency-first mindset; the resulting talent mobility may generate selective recruitment opportunities in specific functional areas.

For Nike, the core challenge is clear: its globally standardized systems are increasingly insufficient when responding to local agility demands, deepening category specialization, and meeting the new expectations of talent—namely value alignment and accelerated growth.

In response, this report proposes the "Pathfinder Program", recommending that Nike immediately initiate an inside-out organizational transformation through a three-phase approach:

  1. Establishing critical expert roles
  2. Creating innovation special zones
  3. Reframing its employer narrative

The objective is to decisively win the talent war of the next five years.

Key Conclusions

The current competitive landscape represents an ultimate contest of organizational models.

  • ANTA cultivates "insiders" through systems.
  • adidas unites "strivers" through culture.
  • On attracts "believers" through conviction.

Nike must resolve the inherent paradox between "global standards" and "local agility." The answer is not simple poaching, but rather the deliberate design of a more compelling new working model for its target talent segments.

1. ANTA Group vs. Nike: Systemic Symbiosis vs. Brand Halo

ANTA has built an internally generated talent system that is exceptionally difficult to replicate. Its core is not aggressive external hiring through premium compensation, but the long-cycle cultivation of backbone talent imbued with "ANTA DNA" over a ten-year horizon.

Dimension ANTA Group Nike Implications and Challenges for Nike
Strategic Core A "symbiotic value" system. Employees are regarded as long-term business partners. Through ultra-long-term investment, ANTA aligns individual career trajectories with corporate growth, creating resonance over time. Brand-driven attraction underpinned by DEI (Diversity, Equity & Inclusion). Leverages global brand power and inclusive values to attract talent worldwide. Challenge: ANTA's system produces highly loyal mid-level management. Nike finds it extremely difficult to poach ANTA's core leaders, as what binds them is not only compensation, but predictable advancement and holistic life support.
Talent Development "High-Middle-Junior three-tier engine" + "Stars Program." Tailored development paths for different career stages. Campus hires rotate across brands and functions, breaking early specialization. Nearly 40% of graduates from the Olympic Stars Program have advanced into mid-to-senior management roles. Global standardized training and leadership development programs. Mature global frameworks emphasize leadership capability and functional excellence within a unified system. Insight: Nike needs more China battle-tested acceleration programs. For high-potential talent, Nike could design local critical-role rotations spanning market, supply chain, and digital functions to rapidly produce hybrid leaders.
Incentives & Retention A 36-item welfare system that forms a powerful moat. Benefits include interest-free first-home loans, employee apartments, and children's education support. Investment exceeded RMB 400 million in 2024. Competitive global compensation and benefits aligned with international standards, enhanced by sports allowances and social impact initiatives. Challenge: ANTA's benefits directly address China's core life pressures (housing, children). These are significantly more adhesive than salary alone. Nike's global benefits package requires sharper local adaptation.
Employer Image "A stable, long-term career partner." Emphasizes shared growth and comprehensive security, highly attractive to top local talent seeking steady progression. "A global sports leader and innovator." Naturally appealing to elite talent aspiring to international exposure and innovation-driven culture. Insight: Nike should amplify the narrative of "Defining the future from China." Position China not merely as an execution hub, but as an innovation origin influencing global products.

Core Distinction: ANTA is a system-driven ecosystem builder, while Nike is a brand-driven innovation leader. ANTA secures its talent foundation through internal cultivation and deep welfare integration; Nike attracts elite talent through global brand equity and innovation culture.

Market Actions & Strategic Orientation

Products: ANTA executes a "single focus, multi-brand, omnichannel" strategy.

  • The core brand anchors mass professional sports.
  • FILA consolidates leadership in fashion sportswear.
  • Arc'teryx, DESCENTE and others dominate premium professional segments. Group-level operations enable R&D and supply-chain synergies.

Channels: Omnichannel coordination with optimized offline store tiering and differentiated online brand operations. A powerful distribution system enables deep penetration across all market tiers.

Marketing: Independent brand narratives.

  • ANTA emphasizes "Love Sports."
  • FILA focuses on fashion.
  • Arc'teryx builds elite professional communities. Marketing investment prioritizes long-term brand equity and market share over short-term ROI.

Organizational Structure & Decision-Making

Multi-brand autonomy with strong group synergy: Each brand retains significant operational independence, while the group provides centralized strength in supply chain, retail management, and digital platforms—forming a federal-style advantage.

Highly institutionalized and process-driven: Standardized systems govern the entire career lifecycle—development, promotion, and welfare—ensuring operational stability.

Founder vision combined with professional management: The founding family steers strategic direction, while seasoned professional managers execute day-to-day operations, resulting in mature corporate governance.

Talent Strategy Core

"Internal first, external selectively": Large-scale campus hiring and management trainee programs (e.g., Stars Program, Olympic Stars Program) form the backbone of talent development. External hiring is decisive but limited to scarce, high-end profiles such as luxury retail or niche-category experts.

Deep-binding through welfare architecture: The 36-item welfare system addresses long-term life needs, building loyalty beyond monetary compensation.

Talent Profile: Prefers individuals who identify with group culture, value stable growth, and demonstrate strong collaboration—career partners rather than short-term opportunists.

Employer Value Proposition (EVP)

"Join us as a long-term career partner—grow together, with comprehensive and lasting security."

Talent Acquisition Strategy for Nike

Challenge: ANTA has cultivated a cohort of highly loyal mid-to-senior leaders fluent in complex multi-brand operations. They are deeply embedded and resistant to conventional role- or pay-based poaching.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Target: Select individuals within premium brands (e.g., Arc'teryx, DESCENTE) who possess international exposure or aspire to a broader innovation platform.
  • Value Proposition: Offer a leap from operating a successful brand to shaping the future of global sport. Leverage Nike's frontier technology initiatives and unmatched global influence to attract those who feel constrained by systemic rigidity and seek larger, more ambitious challenges.
2. adidas vs. Nike: Cultural Belonging vs. Elite Performance Orientation

adidas has cultivated a strong sense of local belonging by creating best-in-class office environments and an inclusive culture. Its talent strategy is designed to attract and retain professionals who are willing to grow and strive within a structured system.

Dimension adidas Nike Implications and Challenges for Nike
Strategic Core "Homecourt" culture and employee experience. By investing heavily in world-class office environments (e.g., Shanghai HQ), inclusive culture, and rich employee activities, adidas fosters a strong sense of belonging. Sports-driven spirit and performance orientation. Culture emphasizes competition, breakthrough, and winning, with a stronger elite mindset and results pressure. Challenge: adidas offers a more comfortable, supportive day-to-day work experience. When competing for similarly capable professional managers, this becomes a critical soft differentiator.
Organizational Climate Emphasis on diversity, inclusion, and teamwork. Global Inclusion Weeks and similar initiatives create an open, fair environment that encourages diversity of thought. Emphasis on competition, excellence, and individual breakthroughs. The culture is more aggressive, with a tendency toward individual heroism, generating higher internal tension. Insight: While maintaining a "win" culture, Nike needs to place greater emphasis on team collaboration and sustainable growth. adidas's internal community-building practices offer useful reference points.
Talent Attraction "Top Employer" branding with strong localization commitment. Repeatedly recognized as a "China Top Employer," signaling long-term investment in the China market and appealing to candidates seeking a stable global platform. The halo of a global innovation benchmark. Exceptionally attractive to top-tier design, marketing, and product innovation talent—symbolizing the pinnacle of the industry. Challenge: In non-elite creative roles (e.g., retail operations, regional management), adidas's employer awards and superior work experience offer broader appeal.
Career Pathways Structured and visible career development tracks. Clear promotion pathways and strong training support enable long-term internal growth. Fast-track programs and global opportunities. High performers may advance rapidly or rotate internationally, offering steep but uncertain growth trajectories. Insight: Nike should design more visible and stable medium-to-long-term career roadmaps for China-based talent, clarifying milestones toward senior local leadership roles to reduce attrition driven by uncertainty.

Core Distinction: adidas is a model student of localization, while Nike is a global leader. adidas seeks deep integration into China; Nike seeks to lead China.

Market Actions & Strategic Orientation

Products: The "In China, for China" strategy is deeply embedded. The Shanghai design center plays a pivotal role, leading localized design for franchises such as Originals. Product iteration is fast and closely aligned with Chinese trends.

Channels: Balanced omnichannel development. Offline store experience upgrades continue, while online collaboration with platforms such as Douyin is deep. Wholesale and direct-to-consumer relationships remain relatively stable.

Marketing: Strong alignment with local celebrities and cultural IPs. Investment in core sports such as football and running remains consistent and disciplined.

Organizational Structure & Decision-Making

Matrix structure with strong local empowerment: Within the Global–APAC–China matrix, China leadership enjoys substantial decision-making authority, particularly in marketing and product design.

Process rigor balanced with flexibility: German-style process discipline is maintained, while tactical flexibility is allowed in response to local market dynamics.

Employee-experience driven: Significant investment in office environments, employee benefits, and team-building initiatives to cultivate a "home-like" atmosphere.

Talent Strategy Core

Systematic cultivation of retail excellence: The Retail Elite Development Program is a benchmark initiative, designed to develop management trainees into store managers within 24 months, solving the scalable supply of retail management talent.

Cultural fit and belonging as retention anchors: Recruitment places heavy emphasis on cultural alignment with diversity, inclusion, and collaboration. Retention strategies focus on long-term relationships and emotional bonds.

Talent Profile: Professionals seeking both international exposure and local commitment. Preference for collaborative individuals who adapt well to processes and value work–life balance.

Employer Value Proposition (EVP)

"Here, you belong to a global family—while deeply cultivating your impact in the China market."

Talent Acquisition Strategy for Nike

Challenge: In functions such as retail management, supply chain, and local marketing, adidas offers a platform comparable to Nike's—but with a gentler culture and clearer, more predictable career paths.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Target: Frontline retail trainers, regional retail managers, and senior designers and product marketers from the Shanghai design center.
  • Value Proposition: For talent seeking faster breakthroughs and higher innovation ceilings, emphasize Nike's culture of challenging conventions and steeper growth curves—backed by more demanding, high-impact projects and greater creative latitude.
3. LI-NING vs. Nike: Agility and Flexibility vs. Systemic Standardization

LI-NING represents the most agile and execution-driven organizational archetype among China's domestic sportswear brands. Its talent strategy is explicitly designed to support rapid decision-making, high-frequency iteration, and immediate market responsiveness.

Dimension LI-NING Nike Implications and Challenges for Nike
Strategic Core Extreme pragmatism and speed. Business decisions prioritize short feedback loops and quick validation, enabling fast adaptation to market signals. Long-cycle global planning. Strategy is formulated through multilayered global processes, ensuring consistency but reducing responsiveness. Challenge: Nike's decision-making cadence often lags behind fast-moving local trends. Against LI-NING, speed itself becomes a competitive disadvantage.
Organizational Structure Highly flattened. Clear accountability, minimal hierarchy, and strong empowerment of frontline teams. Matrix-based global organization. Multiple reporting lines and approval layers ensure alignment but increase friction. Insight: Nike could pilot "China rapid-response squads" with simplified approval mechanisms for selected categories or channels.
Incentive Mechanisms Strong performance linkage. Bonuses and promotions are tightly coupled with short-term business outcomes, creating a high-pressure, high-reward environment. Balanced incentive design. Performance is evaluated through a mix of financial results, leadership behaviors, and long-term impact. Challenge: For execution-oriented talent, Nike's incentive feedback may feel distant or abstract compared with LI-NING's immediate rewards.
Talent Attraction Appeals to doers and operators. Highly attractive to individuals who value autonomy, speed, and tangible results over process sophistication. Appeals to strategists and innovators. Strong draw for talent seeking structured development and global exposure. Insight: Nike should differentiate its EVP by clearly signaling which roles offer true end-to-end ownership and rapid decision authority.

Core Distinction: LI-NING is a battlefield organization, optimized for speed and execution; Nike is a system organization, optimized for scale, consistency, and long-term brand leadership.

Market Actions & Strategic Orientation

Products: LI-NING aggressively leverages national pride and Chinese cultural symbolism, rapidly launching trend-responsive products tied to domestic events and consumer sentiment. Product cycles are short, and iteration is constant.

Channels: Strong focus on DTC and online platforms, particularly Tmall and Douyin. Channel strategies shift quickly in response to traffic and conversion data.

Marketing: Highly event-driven and opportunistic. Marketing investments pivot rapidly around sports events, social trends, and viral opportunities.

Organizational Structure & Decision-Making

Founder-led and highly centralized vision: Founder influence remains strong, enabling swift strategic pivots without prolonged internal debate.

Lean headquarters, strong frontline empowerment: HQ sets direction; execution is pushed aggressively to brand, product, and channel teams.

Tolerance for experimentation—and failure: Trial-and-error is embedded in daily operations, fostering a culture of rapid learning.

Talent Strategy Core

Results-first talent philosophy: LI-NING values immediate contribution over pedigree. Past success in driving sales, channels, or campaigns outweighs formal background.

Flexible compensation and promotion: High performers can achieve accelerated advancement and outsized rewards, while underperformers are quickly filtered out.

Talent Profile: Entrepreneurial, resilient, execution-heavy professionals who thrive under pressure and ambiguity.

Employer Value Proposition (EVP)

"If you can deliver results fast, you will be rewarded faster."

Talent Acquisition Strategy for Nike

Challenge: Execution-focused professionals may perceive Nike as slow-moving, overly process-heavy, and distant from frontline business impact.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Target: High-performing category managers, channel operators, and growth marketers who have proven their ability to scale success beyond rapid wins.
  • Value Proposition: Position Nike as the platform where battle-hardened executors can elevate into system builders—moving from short-term victories to shaping enduring brands and categories at scale.
4. On vs. Nike: Belief-Driven Organization vs. System-Driven Organization

On represents a new-generation sportswear brand whose talent strategy is built almost entirely on professional belief and product conviction. Compared with traditional giants, its organizational model is deliberately minimalist, relying on shared values rather than institutional density.

Dimension On Nike Implications and Challenges for Nike
Strategic Core Product belief and mission alignment. Talent is attracted primarily by faith in the product, technology, and brand vision rather than scale or stability. Institutional excellence and global leadership. Talent is attracted by systems, resources, and global influence. Challenge: On competes for the same top-tier product and innovation talent, but with a radically different emotional proposition—belief over structure.
Organizational Structure Ultra-flat and founder-influenced. Decision-making is highly concentrated among senior product and brand leaders. Multi-layered global matrix. Decisions balance global alignment and local execution. Insight: Nike's layered structure may dilute product passion. Creating small, founder-like teams within Nike could restore emotional ownership.
Talent Motivation Intrinsic motivation dominates. Individuals are driven by craftsmanship, innovation, and the desire to build something meaningful from the ground up. Extrinsic and intrinsic motivations coexist. Compensation, brand prestige, and innovation opportunities are balanced. Challenge: For true believers, Nike's scale may feel impersonal; passion risks being diluted by process.
Employer Appeal A cult-like attraction among elite product innovators. On is perceived as authentic, pure, and uncompromised. A benchmark platform for industry excellence. Nike represents the pinnacle of professional achievement. Insight: Nike must articulate how scale and belief can coexist—innovation with impact, not innovation diluted.

Core Distinction: On is a belief-centric organization—small, focused, and conviction-driven. Nike is a system-centric organization—powerful, scalable, and institutionally robust.

Market Actions & Strategic Orientation

Products: On maintains obsessive focus on core running technology and footwear innovation. Product lines are intentionally narrow, allowing extreme depth rather than breadth.

Channels: Selective distribution with strong DTC emphasis. Scarcity and curation reinforce brand purity.

Marketing: Story-driven and founder-led. Marketing highlights athlete stories, product breakthroughs, and community credibility rather than mass exposure.

Organizational Structure & Decision-Making

Founder proximity to core decisions: Founders and early leaders remain deeply involved in product and brand decisions, preserving authenticity.

Minimal bureaucracy: Processes exist only where they enable speed and quality; everything else is intentionally stripped away.

High tolerance for ambiguity: Roles are fluid, and individuals often wear multiple hats.

Talent Strategy Core

Belief-first hiring: Cultural and product belief outweigh experience or brand pedigree. Passion is the primary screening criterion.

Equity and long-term upside: Equity participation and long-term value creation play a central role in retention.

Talent Profile: Product purists, innovation believers, and builders who value meaning over security.

Employer Value Proposition (EVP)

"Build what you truly believe in—with people who believe as deeply as you do."

Talent Acquisition Strategy for Nike

Challenge: Nike risks losing elite product innovators who seek purity, autonomy, and deep emotional connection to the product.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Target: Senior designers, advanced materials experts, and category innovation leaders who desire scale without surrendering belief.
  • Value Proposition: Offer belief at scale—the chance to turn conviction into global impact, supported by unmatched R&D, athlete insight, and platform power.
5. Xtep vs. Nike: Vertical Specialization vs. Horizontal Expansion

Xtep has deliberately chosen a highly focused vertical strategy, anchoring itself firmly in the running category. Its talent strategy, organizational design, and resource allocation all serve a single objective: becoming the most professional and credible running brand in China.

Dimension Xtep Nike Implications and Challenges for Nike
Strategic Core Extreme category focus. All major decisions revolve around running—products, athletes, events, and communities. Multi-category global leadership. Nike competes across a broad portfolio including running, basketball, football, training, and lifestyle. Challenge: Xtep's singular focus enables deeper expertise and faster accumulation of category authority, particularly in China's mass and semi-professional running segments.
Organizational Model Category-centric vertical integration. Product, marketing, athlete relations, and community operations are tightly aligned around running. Matrix-based category management. Categories compete for resources within a global structure. Insight: Nike could further empower category-specific China teams with end-to-end ownership to counter Xtep's depth advantage.
Talent Specialization Deep cultivation of running specialists. Long-term accumulation of know-how across footwear biomechanics, runner communities, and race ecosystems. Broad-based professional development. Talent is often rotated across categories and functions. Challenge: Nike's rotation system may dilute category depth for highly specialized professionals.
Employer Appeal "The home of runners." Strong emotional resonance with running professionals and enthusiasts. "The ultimate sports platform." Appeals to multi-sport and cross-category talent. Insight: For pure running experts, Nike must clarify why a broader platform still offers the deepest running impact.

Core Distinction: Xtep is a category monolith, building unassailable depth in one vertical. Nike is a category orchestrator, leveraging scale across multiple sports.

Market Actions & Strategic Orientation

Products: Xtep invests heavily in running shoe technology, carbon plate innovation, and marathon-ready product lines. Product validation is closely tied to race performance and athlete feedback.

Channels: Strong presence in running-specialty stores and race venues. Community-based retail plays a significant role in brand building.

Marketing: Deep integration with marathon events, running clubs, and grassroots races. Marketing credibility is earned through performance rather than celebrity endorsement.

Organizational Structure & Decision-Making

Running-first decision hierarchy: All major investments are evaluated through their contribution to the running ecosystem.

Close integration with athlete communities: Internal teams maintain direct, frequent contact with runners, coaches, and event organizers.

Operational discipline with category obsession: Processes exist to serve category excellence, not corporate formality.

Talent Strategy Core

Category immersion as a career path: Talent grows by staying within the running domain for extended periods, accumulating irreplaceable expertise.

Community-driven retention: Belonging to the running ecosystem—athletes, events, clubs—creates strong emotional stickiness.

Talent Profile: Running specialists, biomechanical experts, event operators, and community builders with deep category passion.

Employer Value Proposition (EVP)

"If running defines who you are, this is where your career belongs."

Talent Acquisition Strategy for Nike

Challenge: Nike risks being perceived as too broad for specialists who seek deep category immersion and visible authority within a single sport.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Target: Senior running footwear designers, performance marketers, and athlete managers seeking global reach beyond China.
  • Value Proposition: Position Nike as the platform where deep specialization meets global amplification—allowing running experts to influence not just one market, but the future of running worldwide.
6. PEAK vs. Nike: Efficiency-First Orientation vs. Long-Termism

PEAK has, in recent years, undergone a strategic recalibration toward efficiency, cost discipline, and focused returns. Its talent strategy reflects this shift, emphasizing operational effectiveness over expansive organizational ambition.

Dimension PEAK Nike Implications and Challenges for Nike
Strategic Core Efficiency and cost control. Business decisions prioritize ROI, operational leverage, and disciplined resource allocation. Long-term brand investment and category leadership. Strategic patience underpins innovation and global influence. Insight: PEAK's strategy reduces complexity and stabilizes margins, but limits its ability to attract top-tier visionary talent.
Organizational Ambition Selective contraction and focus. Non-core initiatives are streamlined or exited to preserve efficiency. Continuous expansion and reinvention. Nike sustains long-term bets across innovation, sustainability, and digital transformation. Challenge: Nike's long-term investments demand patience from talent; PEAK's shorter cycles appeal to pragmatists.
Talent Philosophy Utility-driven. Talent is valued primarily for immediate operational contribution. Potential-driven. Talent is cultivated for future leadership and innovation impact. Insight: Nike should clearly differentiate roles that require visionary capacity from those that reward operational excellence.
Employer Appeal Stability, predictability, and clear expectations. Appeals to professionals seeking clarity and work discipline. Purpose, ambition, and global scale. Appeals to individuals seeking long-term impact and industry leadership. Challenge: PEAK may become a talent reservoir for Nike in operationally mature roles.

Core Distinction: PEAK is an efficiency optimizer, prioritizing discipline and focus. Nike is a long-term value architect, prioritizing endurance, innovation, and brand legacy.

Market Actions & Strategic Orientation

Products: PEAK concentrates on commercially proven categories, reducing experimentation risk and emphasizing manufacturability and cost efficiency.

Channels: Wholesale-driven with selective DTC presence. Channel strategies emphasize stability over aggressive expansion.

Marketing: Prudent and performance-focused. Marketing investment is tightly controlled and closely tied to sales outcomes.

Organizational Structure & Decision-Making

Lean management layers: Reduced hierarchy and streamlined reporting lines enhance execution speed.

Centralized cost oversight: Strong financial governance governs project approvals and headcount expansion.

Risk-averse culture: Innovation is incremental rather than disruptive.

Talent Strategy Core

Efficiency-aligned hiring: Preference for professionals with strong execution records, cost awareness, and process discipline.

Limited long-term upside narratives: Career growth paths are clear but capped; rapid ascension or global exposure is rare.

Talent Profile: Operations managers, sourcing experts, and execution-focused leaders who value clarity and efficiency.

Employer Value Proposition (EVP)

"Operate with discipline, deliver with efficiency."

Talent Acquisition Strategy for Nike

Challenge: While PEAK is unlikely to compete with Nike for top innovation talent, its recent organizational tightening may release capable operational leaders into the market.

Acquisition Strategy:

  • Target: Supply chain leaders, manufacturing optimization experts, and cost-control specialists seeking a broader strategic canvas.
  • Value Proposition: Offer a transition from efficiency execution to value creation at scale, enabling operational experts to contribute to long-term brand and innovation strategies.
